Lesson: Animals and Adaptations

What are Animal Adaptations?

Adaptations are special characteristics or behaviors that help animals survive and thrive in their environment. These can include physical features like fur color, body size, or special abilities such as camouflage, flight, or sharp teeth.

Types of Adaptations

  • Structural Adaptations: These are physical features of an organism, such as the shape of its beak, the color of its fur, or the type of skin it has. For example, a camel’s hump stores water, which helps it survive in desert conditions.
  • Behavioral Adaptations: These are actions or behaviors that help animals survive. For example, birds migrating to warmer climates during the winter is a behavioral adaptation.
  • Physiological Adaptations: These involve internal body processes, like the ability of some animals to regulate body temperature or change their metabolism in response to environmental changes.
