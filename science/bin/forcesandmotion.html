Lesson: Forces and Motion

What are Forces?

A force is a push or pull on an object that can cause it to accelerate. Forces can be caused by a variety of things, such as gravity, friction, or applied forces.

Newton's Laws of Motion

Sir Isaac Newton's Three Laws of Motion describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting on it.

  • First Law (Law of Inertia): An object at rest will stay at rest, and an object in motion will stay in motion unless acted upon by an external force.
  • Second Law (F=ma): The force acting on an object is equal to the mass of the object times its acceleration.
  • Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Types of Forces

There are many types of forces, including:

  • Gravitational Force: The force that attracts objects towards the center of the Earth.
  • Friction: A force that opposes the motion of an object.
  • Applied Force: A force applied to an object, such as pushing or pulling.
  • Normal Force: The force exerted by a surface to support the weight of an object resting on it.
