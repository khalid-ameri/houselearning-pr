Circles: Key Concepts 🟡

Circles have unique properties. The **circumference** is the distance around the circle (like the perimeter of a square), and the **area** is the space inside the circle.

  The **radius (r)** is the distance from the center to any point on the edge. The **diameter (d)** is the distance across the circle through the center.   $$d = 2r$$

  **Pi ($\pi$)** is a special number, approximately **3.14159**, which represents the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.
