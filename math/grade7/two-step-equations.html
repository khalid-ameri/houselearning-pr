Solving Two-Step Equations

A **two-step equation** requires two operations to solve for the unknown variable. The key is to follow a specific order to isolate the variable.

  The rule of thumb is to **undo addition/subtraction first**, then **undo multiplication/division**. This is the reverse of the order of operations (PEMDAS)!   $$2x + 5 = 17$$   **Step 1:** Undo the addition. Subtract 5 from both sides.   $$2x + 5 - 5 = 17 - 5$$   $$2x = 12$$   **Step 2:** Undo the multiplication. Divide both sides by 2.   $$\frac{2x}{2} = \frac{12}{2}$$   $$x = 6$$
